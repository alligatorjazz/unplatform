Hello all -- checking in on the blog again to let folks know about a few events coming up.

## Virtual Event: Unplatform Office Hours - May 14th, 2026 @ 7:00pm 

I get a lot of questions from folks around the world about how best to "unplatform" their communities--questions about what tech stacks to use, where to promote events, and how to even start the "unplatforming" conversation with their friends and peers in the first place. Up to now I've basically answered these questions as they come up over email or call, but I get enough of them now that I think it would be better to have an event where folks can come together to talk about these issues and share answers of their own. Enter: Unplatform Office Hours, a monthly virtual event where I'll hop on a call to give (and receive) advice about how to reclaim tech sovereignty.

Those who know me IRL will know that I've been hosting events like this in the NYC area, and the conversations I've had at those events have been invaluable for informing the future direction of the Unplatform project and have been great for learning how to turn tech-critical theory into practice. I encourage everyone reading this who's interested in finding alternative to Big Tech platforms to come, and I *especially* encourage you to invite folks who might be tech-critical-curious but who haven't had the time or confidence to go through the full Unplatform guide yet.

[You can RSVP here.](https://luma.com/ntlx63og) If you can't make that time, [feel free to shoot me an email.](mailto:unplatform@fromthesuperhighway.com) My inbox remains open.

## IRL Event: Unplatform Creatives Workshop #6 - May 28th, 2026 @ 6:30pm (875 3rd Ave, Midtown NYC)

I'm also using this post as an opportunity to let NYC-based folks know about the next IRL workshop, which will be on May 28th at the [privately-owned-public-space](https://apops.mas.org/about/what-are-pops/) at 875 3rd Avenue. These in-person workshops have already been the seeding ground for several IRL organizing projects, and my hope is that they can seed even more before the season is out. I'm interpreting "creative" very broadly here: the attendees have ranged from musicians to woodworkers to documentarians. If you're thinking "I don't know if I'm creative enough to come," you definitely are, and you should definitely pull up.

[You can RSVP to this event here.](https://luma.com/6z5j4bxi) Additionally, I would love to get a network of these in-person meetups going in cities around the world. If you're interested in setting up your own Unplatform meetup, [shoot me an email](mailto:unplatform@fromthesuperhighway.com). (I'd even be willing to come and help you in-person if you're within a train ride's distance of NYC).
